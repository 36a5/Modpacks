# Shabab 2 — What's Actually In This Pack

Most people on this server play the Solo Leveling half — level up, run Gates, fight for a job — and
never touch anything else. That's a shame, because it's maybe a fifth of what's installed.

There are **seven other worlds** you can walk to, a dozen bosses that will kill you, and a map full
of dungeons nobody has opened. This is the tour.

> For levelling, jobs, Gates, Epic Fight, MineColonies, vehicles, voice chat and keybinds, see the
> **[Systems Guide](systems.md)**. This page is only about *places to go and things to kill*.

---

## 🌍 Dimensions — the seven other worlds

### ☁️ The Aether
The classic sky dimension. Floating islands, flying pigs you can ride, dungeons in the clouds.

**How to get there:** build a Nether-portal-shaped frame out of **Glowstone**, then pour a **bucket
of water** into it.

Massively expanded here by **Aether: Redux**, **Deep Aether** (new biomes, a new dungeon, and the
Skyjade and Stratus ore/armour tiers), **Aether Villages**, and **Lost Content**. This is not the
tiny Aether you may remember — it's a full progression on its own.

### 🌲 The Twilight Forest
Permanently dusk, enormous trees, and a strict boss ladder. Probably the best-designed content in
the whole pack.

**How to get there:** dig a **2×2 pool of water**, ring it with dirt/grass/podzol blocks that have
**flowers or mushrooms** planted on top, then **throw a diamond into the water** (`Q`). Lightning
strikes and the portal opens.

The Twilight Forest is *gated*: each boss drops the progression that unlocks the next area. Start
with the **Naga** (in the Naga Courtyard), then the **Lich** (Lich Tower). Don't wander into a
Dark Forest early — the mod will tell you you're not ready, and it means it.

### 🌗 Blue Skies — the Everbright & the Everdawn
Two dimensions, one bright and one dark, each with its own tower dungeons and bosses.

**How to get there:** find a **Gatekeeper**'s house (plains, mountains, or snowy biomes). He keeps
an **unlit portal frame** in his home and sells a **Zeal Lighter** for emeralds. Buy the lighter,
light his frame, walk through. Which of the two dimensions you get depends on which frame his house
generated with — find another Gatekeeper for the other one.

### 🕳️ The Otherside (Deeper and Darker)
What's underneath the Deep Dark. Sculk everything, and it is genuinely dangerous.

**How to get there:** find an **Ancient City** (Deep Dark, around Y **-52**). The big Reinforced
Deepslate archway in the middle *is* the portal frame. To light it you need a **Heart of the Deep**,
which only drops from **killing the Warden**. Clear any sculk off the frame, then right-click it with
the Heart.

**Write your coordinates down before you go through.** The Otherside is huge, and if you lose the
portal the only way home is dying.

### 🍄 The Undergarden
A vast, glowing underground world. Poisonous, but rich.

**How to get there:** craft a **Catalyst** (4 iron ingots + 4 gold ingots + 1 diamond). Build a
Nether-portal-shaped frame out of **Stone Bricks** or **Deepslate Bricks** (minimum 4×5), then
**right-click the bottom inner block of the frame** with the Catalyst. It turns green.

It only works in the **Overworld** — a frame built in the Nether or End will not light.

### 🏙️ The Lost Cities
An entire world of ruined skyscrapers. No terrain, just a dead city stretching to the horizon,
stuffed with loot and infested.

**How to get there:** it's a ritual, not a portal. Place a **bed on top of two Diamond Blocks**,
surround the bed with **6 skulls** (any type), and then **sleep in it**.

*(The "block under the bed" is a config value in this mod and our pack leaves it at the default,
which is the diamond block. If it doesn't fire, check your skull placement first.)*

### ⚔️ The Solo Leveling Gates
The Gate dimensions you already know — see the **[Systems Guide](systems.md#-solo-leveling--gates-levels--jobs)**.

### Not dimensions (common confusion)
- **BetterEnd** and **BetterNether** completely rewrite the *vanilla* End and Nether. Same portals,
  unrecognisable worlds. Go and look again.
- **Deep Aether** is inside the Aether, not next to it.
- **Umbral Skies** is an Aether × Twilight Forest crossover — boss trophies and gloves. Not a place.

## 💀 Bosses

> ⚠️ **Bosses are a progression now — they get harder the further out you go.** Overworld minibosses
> are the entry rung; each dimension's ladder is tougher than the last; the Ender Dragon is the wall
> at the end (**100,000 HP**). You are meant to upgrade your gear and level up to keep pace.
>
> Rough health: overworld minibosses **1,000–2,000**, the Wither **4,000**, the Warden **6,000**;
> Twilight Forest ramps **2,000 → 26,000** (Naga easiest, Snow Queen hardest); the Aether, Cataclysm
> and beyond climb to **40,000–90,000**; the **Ender Dragon 100,000**. Boss damage scales the same way.
>
> **Killing a boss gives Solo Leveling XP — to your whole party.** Everyone partied (`P`) and nearby
> gains **5 XP per point of the boss's health**: a 1,000 HP miniboss = 5,000 XP, the 150,000 HP Ender
> Dragon = **750,000**. Boss-hunting *is* how you level. (Solo Leveling XP is also 3× faster on top.)
>
> **→ [Full boss list — HP, damage and rewards](boss-rewards.md)**
> **→ [Progression route — what to fight first, and what armour to wear](progression.md)**
>
> **Every boss drops a bounty at the corpse, scaled to its health** — grind materials you'd normally
> mine for:
> - **Any miniboss (1k+):** diamonds, emeralds, amethyst, XP bottles. A ~1k boss ≈ **5 diamonds**;
>   bigger bosses give proportionally more.
> - **Real bosses (5k+):** also ancient debris + netherite scrap.
> - **Big bosses (8k+ / 10k+):** also echo shards, then enchanted golden apples.
>
> So minibosses are worth killing, and the rare materials still mean you fought something.
>
> **Bonus rewards:**
> - **First kill** of each boss type pays **double levels** — hunt the full list.
> - **Deathless fight** (nobody in the party dies) — **+50% levels and bounty**.
> - **50k+ HP bosses** get their kill **announced server-wide with fireworks**, and pay raw **SP**
>   on top — so even maxed players want them.
>
> **And you take half damage from all mobs now** — the fights are hard, not unfair.
>
> **Tip — `/invest 50`:** choose how many points each `+` click in the stats panel spends, so
> re-assigning a Reborn Elixir refund takes seconds, not six hundred clicks.
>
> **The loot is worth it.** A scaled boss drops **5× the materials**, big XP, and any weapon or armour
> it drops comes out **enchanted past the vanilla ceiling** — a Power V bow off the Hydra lands as
> **Power VIII**. Boss gear beats anything you can craft.
>
> **Shadow Monarchs: your army is a reflection of you.** **Igris, Tusk and Beru get HALF your max
> health and armour; every other shadow gets a quarter** — so building yourself up builds them up.
> Their **damage scales with your Intelligence**: go all-in on INT and your shadows become the
> weapon. The army re-syncs to you every few seconds.
>
> **Respec when you want:** craft a **Reborn Elixir** (a Class Selector ringed by 8 ancient debris —
> search "reborn" in JEI) and use it — every stat point you've ever allocated returns to SP, ready
> to re-spend in the panel (`;`). Level, rank and job are untouched.
>
> Only **Solo Leveling's own bosses** are left at their normal numbers.
>
> These are not solo fights. Bring a party, bring potions, and expect to die.

### L_Ender's Cataclysm — the big one
Eight bosses, each in its own hand-built arena, each dropping a weapon that's worth the fight.

| Boss | Where | How it starts |
|---|---|---|
| **Netherite Monstrosity** | Nether, in its arena | Spawns naturally in the structure |
| **Ignis** | The Burning Arena | Kill the Ignited Revenants upstairs for **Burning Ashes**, then right-click the ashes on the **Altar of Fire** |
| **Ender Guardian** | Lowest floor of its arena | Walk up to the altar. It breaks the floor and drops you into the real arena |
| **The Leviathan** | Sunken City | Right-click an **Abyssal Sacrifice** into the **Altar of the Abyss** |
| **Maledictus** | Its grave | Right-click the grave |
| **The Harbinger**, **Ancient Remnant**, **Scylla** | Their own structures | Found by exploring |

Cataclysm armour has **abilities on keys**, and they were moved off Solo Leveling's keys:
`[` ability · `]` helmet · `,` chestplate · `.` boots.

### Bosses'Rise — souls-like
Five bosses in their own dungeons: the **Infernal Dragon**, the **Yeti**, the **Sand Worm**, the
**Underworld Knight** and one more. They chain — an item dropped by one is the key to summoning the
next.

> ⚠️ **Its Dodge Roll is currently unbound.** These are souls-like fights designed around dodging.
> Bind it: `Esc → Options → Controls → Dodge Roll`. You will want it.

### Mowzie's Mobs
**Ferrous Wroughtnaut** (locked in the Wrought Chamber underground), **Frostmaw** (sleeping in snowy
mountains — it's the big pile of fur), **Barako the Sun Chief** (atop a Barakoa village), and the
**Naga**. All hand-animated and all much harder than they look.

### The Conjurer
An Illager magician with a **theatre in the Dark/Roofed Forest**. Fight through the pillagers inside,
and he starts his show. A good first "real" boss.

### Born in Chaos
A horror mod. **Sir Pumpkinhead** (mid-game) and **Lord Pumpkinhead** (end-game) are multi-phase
seasonal bosses; **Fallen Chaos Knight** and **Nightmare Stalker** are also in here. Some of its
invaders show up on specific in-game days, some are summoned from structures you find.

### Mutant Monsters
Mutant Zombie, Mutant Skeleton, Mutant Creeper, Mutant Enderman. Not bosses exactly — they just
appear, and they will end you if you treat them like the normal version.

### Dragons
**Dragon Mounts: Legacy** — hatch a dragon egg and ride it. The Ender Dragon also drops much more
here (**EnderDragon Loot**).

**The Ender Dragon is now a four-phase fight** (Shabab 2 datapack, no mod to install):
- **75% health** — *first blood*: a flight of Shadow phantoms drops onto everyone fighting.
- **50% health** — *enrage*: a bigger phantom wave, plus a **Void barrage** that rains fire straight
  down on random players — keep moving.
- **25% health** — *second wind*: the dragon heals hard **once**, and the **Herald of the Void**
  answers — a netherite-clad mini-boss that hits like a truck and **always drops its sword** (its
  armour has a chance too). Kill the Herald, then finish the dragon.

## 🐺 Mobs

- **Alex's Mobs** — the big one. ~80 new animals, all over the world, most with a use. Grizzly bears,
  elephants, mimicubes, void worms, the lot.
- **Friends & Foes** — the mobs Mojang voted down and then abandoned: the Copper Golem, Moobloom,
  Iceologer, Barnacle, Glare, Rascal.
- **Illager Invasion** & **Raided** — raids get much worse. New illager types.
- **Guard Villagers** — villagers you can hire to fight for you.
- **Goblin Traders** — goblins underground who'll trade you things a villager never would.
- **Villagers Plus** — new villager professions.
- **Galosphere** — a whole new cave ecology (silver, allays, sterling).
- **Deeper and Darker** — new sculk mobs, in the Deep Dark and the Otherside.

## 🏰 Dungeons & structures

Every vanilla structure in this pack has been rebuilt, and there are dozens of new ones.

**YUNG's suite** rewrites the classics — **Better Dungeons, Mineshafts, Strongholds, Witch Huts,
Ocean Monuments, Nether Fortresses, Jungle Temples, End Island** and **Cave Biomes**. If you think
you know what a stronghold looks like, you don't.

**New, on top of that:**
- **When Dungeons Arise** — huge, unmistakable landmark dungeons. Airships, mountain keeps, undead
  pirate ships.
- **Dungeons & Taverns** — a proper overhaul of the pillager outpost and friends.
- **Stalwart Dungeons** — combat-focused.
- **Repurposed Structures** — variants of everything, everywhere.
- **Structory** + **Structory: Towers** — small, gorgeous, atmospheric ruins. No loot bloat.
- **Towns & Towers** — villages and outposts, massively expanded.
- **CTOV (Choice Theorem's Overhauled Village)** — every village biome rebuilt properly.
- **Explorations**, **AdoraBuild**, **Moog's** (Voyager / End / Nether / Missing Villages), **Ruins**,
  **The Lost Cities**, **Ancient City Overhaul**.

**Find them:** you have **Xaero's World Map** (`M`) and **Nature's Compass** — craft it, and it will
point you at any biome you name.

## ⚔️ Gear & items worth knowing

- **Boss weapons.** Almost every boss above drops something unique. Cataclysm's are the strongest —
  check its armour tooltips for the ability key (`[`, `]`, `,`, `.`).
- **Advanced Netherite** — tiers past netherite.
- **Charm of Undying** / **Void Totem** — the totem, but it also saves you from the void.
- **Magnum Torch** — stops mob spawns in a huge radius. Build one for your base.
- **Inmis** backpacks, **Nether Chested**, **Elytra Slot** (elytra in its own slot — wear a chestplate
  too), **XP Tome**, **Vein Mining** (hold the key, break a whole ore vein).
- **Shield Expansion** — shields actually do something now.

### How to look anything up
- **JEI** — hover an item, press **`R`** for how to make it, **`U`** for what it makes.
- **Patchouli guidebooks** — several mods ship an in-game manual. If you get one as a drop, read it.
- **Enchantment Descriptions** — hover an enchantment and it tells you what it actually does.

## Where to start tonight

If you've only ever done Solo Leveling and want one thing to go do:

1. **Twilight Forest.** Cheapest portal, best content, and it holds your hand into a real boss ladder.
2. **The Conjurer.** One structure, one fight, good loot, no prep.
3. **Alex's Mobs.** You don't need to go anywhere — just look around properly.
