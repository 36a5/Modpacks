# Progression Route — what to fight, in what order, in what gear

The pack is huge and the bosses are scaled, so wandering into the wrong dimension early is how you
lose an evening. This is the route that works.

> Boss health and rewards are in **[boss-rewards.md](boss-rewards.md)**. Everything you can *do* is in
> **[content.md](content.md)**. Exact armour numbers are always in **JEI** — hover a piece, the
> tooltip is authoritative. Numbers here are the pack's config defaults and an admin can retune them
> live with `/scaling`.

---

## The one thing that changes everything

**Boss health decides the reward.** Every boss pays **5 Solo Leveling XP per point of its health**,
plus an item bounty scaled the same way. So the route is simply: *fight the biggest thing you can
actually survive, then use what it drops to survive something bigger.*

Two multipliers are worth planning around:

- **First kill** of each boss type = **double XP**. Kill each new boss once before farming any of them.
- **Deathless fight** = **+50% XP and bounty**. Bring a party (`P`) and res-rush rather than dying.

## Stage 0 — Awaken, then don't skip the minibosses

Get your Solo Leveling job first (`;` panel). Then, before any portal:

**Fight the overworld minibosses.** They are 800–2,000 HP — killable in iron/diamond — and they pay
real money now: **~5 diamonds per 1,000 HP**, plus emeralds, amethyst and XP bottles.

| Target | HP | Where |
|---|---|---|
| Mutant Creeper / Skeleton / Zombie | 800–1,200 | Overworld, at night |
| Ferrous Wroughtnaut | 1,200 | Underground Wrought Chamber |
| The Conjurer | 900 | Dark Forest theatre |
| Frostmaw | 1,500 | Snowy mountains (the fur pile) |
| Sculptor | 1,600 | — |
| Lord Pumpkinhead | 2,000 | Born in Chaos structure |

This stage is where diamonds stop being a mining problem. **Do not mine for diamonds in this pack** —
kill minibosses instead.

**Gear:** full diamond, Protection IV, then netherite. Advanced Netherite is installed, so netherite
has upgrade tiers past vanilla.

## Stage 1 — Twilight Forest (the real tutorial)

**Enter:** 2×2 water pool, ringed with dirt/grass with flowers on top, throw a diamond in.

Twilight Forest is *gated* — each boss unlocks the next area — and its ladder is the gentlest in the
pack. This is the correct first dimension.

| Order | Boss | HP | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---|
| 1 | **Naga** | 2,000 | Naga Courtyard. Scale armour is your first upgrade. |
| 2 | **Lich** | 3,500 | Lich Tower. |
| 3 | **Minoshroom** | 5,500 | Labyrinth. **5,000+ = ancient debris & netherite scrap start dropping.** |
| 4 | **Hydra** | 8,000 | Hydra Lair. **8,000+ = echo shards.** |
| 5 | **Knight Phantom** | 11,000 | Knight Stronghold. **10,000+ = enchanted golden apples.** |
| 6 | **Ur-Ghast** | 15,000 | Dark Tower. |
| 7 | **Alpha Yeti** | 20,000 | Yeti Cave. |
| 8 | **Snow Queen** | 26,000 | Aurora Palace. |

**Gear from here:** **Fiery** armour (sets attackers alight), **Knightmetal**, then **Phantom** from
the Knight Phantom, **Yeti/Arctic** for the cold areas. Fiery + Phantom carries you out of this
dimension comfortably.

**By the end of Twilight Forest** you should be several hundred Solo Leveling levels up — the ladder
pays roughly 400k XP total.

## Stage 2 — Pick your middle dimension

All three are viable at roughly Twilight-Forest-exit power. Do whichever you like; do all three
eventually, because first kills are double XP.

### The Aether — *the best armour route*
**Enter:** Glowstone frame, pour a water bucket in.

| Boss | HP |
|---|---:|
| Slider (Bronze Dungeon) | 22,000 |
| Valkyrie Queen (Silver) | 32,000 |
| Sun Spirit (Gold) | 42,000 |

Expanded here by **Deep Aether**, **Aether Redux** and **Lost Content**, so it is far bigger than the
old Aether. **This is where the best armour in the pack lives** — see the gear table below.

### Blue Skies — *two dimensions, mid-tier*
**Enter:** find a Gatekeeper's house, buy a Zeal Lighter, light his frame.
Summoner 20k → Starlit Crusher 24k → Alchemist 28k → Arachnarch 32k.

### The Undergarden — *one big fight, good materials*
**Enter:** Catalyst (4 iron + 4 gold + 1 diamond), right-click a Stone/Deepslate Brick frame.
Forgotten Guardian 16,000. Utherium and Froststeel gear.

## Stage 3 — Cataclysm (the difficulty spike)

Cataclysm bosses are scattered across the Overworld/Nether/End rather than one dimension, and they
are where the pack gets serious.

| Order | Boss | HP |
|---|---|---:|
| 1 | Netherite Monstrosity | 12,000 |
| 2 | Ender Guardian | 16,000 |
| 3 | Ignis / Maledictus | 22,000 |
| 4 | The Harbinger / Scylla | 32,000 |
| 5 | **The Leviathan** | 48,000 |
| 6 | **Ancient Remnant** | 75,000 |

**Ignis is the key kill** — **Ignitium** armour is arguably the best in the pack, and the **Ignitium

At **50,000+ HP** (Leviathan, Ancient Remnant) bosses start paying **raw SkillPoints** and their
deaths are announced server-wide with fireworks.

### Enchantments — the part that actually matters

**Boss-dropped gear beats crafted gear, because the pack enchants it past the vanilla ceiling.**
Any weapon or armour a scaled boss drops comes out with **+3 levels on every enchantment it already
had** — a Power V bow lands as **Power VIII**, Protection IV as **Protection VII**. Unenchanted gear
drops enchanted as if from a **level-40** table (vanilla stops at 30), treasure enchants included.

So:
1. **Kill bosses in the armour you have** rather than grinding for a "perfect" crafted set.
2. **Prioritise Protection** on every piece — with the boss bonus it goes past IV.
3. **Mending + Unbreaking III** on anything you like, since durability now costs vanilla rates again.
4. Use the **anvil** to move over-cap enchants onto the set you actually want to wear.

**AttributeFix is installed**, so armour and toughness are not capped at vanilla limits.

## Your stats matter more than your gear

- **Strength directly multiplies the damage you deal** — `1 + Strength × 0.005`, so 1,000 Strength is
  6× and 10,000 is 51×. If you feel weak, this is the stat.
- **Intelligence powers your shadows** — shadow damage is `1 + INT × 0.08`.
- **Shadow Monarchs:** Igris, Tusk and Beru get **half your max health and armour**; every other
  shadow a quarter. Building yourself builds your army.
- **`/invest 50`** — set how many points each `+` click spends, so allocating thousands isn't a
  thousand clicks.
- **Reborn Elixir** (Class Selector + 8 ancient debris) — refunds every stat point so you can respec
  into a Strength or Intelligence build once you know which you want.

**Suggested split:** Strength first until bosses die at a reasonable speed, then Vitality to survive
the 20× hits, then Intelligence if you play Shadow Monarch.

## The short version

1. Get your job. Kill overworld minibosses for diamonds. Netherite up.
2. **Twilight Forest**, in order, Naga → Snow Queen. Wear Fiery, then Phantom.
3. **The Aether** for the best armour. Deep Aether for Stratus/Skyjade.
4. **Cataclysm** — kill Ignis for Ignitium. Then Leviathan, then Ancient Remnant.
5. **Ender Dragon** last, with everyone.

Kill every boss **once** before farming any of them — first kills are worth double.
